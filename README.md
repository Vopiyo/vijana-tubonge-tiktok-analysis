## 📊 Project Overview

This project analyzes the impact of the **Vijana Tubonge** TikTok account in delivering youth-friendly health education, mentorship, and linking adolescents and young people to essential health services in Machakos County, Kenya.

### The Challenge
Adolescents and young people face significant barriers to accessing health services: stigma, distance, lack of awareness. Traditional outreach methods often fail to reach this digitally-native demographic where they spend most of their time—on TikTok.

### The Innovation
Vijana Tubonge (Swahili for "Youth Let's Talk") created a TikTok account to deliver health education through short-form videos, live sessions, and interactive Q&As, meeting young people where they already are.

### The Question
Can a TikTok-based intervention effectively reach, engage, and drive health-seeking behavior among adolescents and young people in a low-resource setting?

### Data Collection
Structured questionnaire covering six domains:
1. Social media usage patterns
2. Exposure and interaction with health content
3. Perceived comfort, safety, and trust
4. Knowledge, attitudes, and behavior change
5. Referral and service utilization
6. Barriers to service access

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 1. Reach & Engagement
- **Peer recommendations (44.0%)** drive most awareness, followed by health workers (24.5%) and TikTok's algorithm (22.3%)
- **84.9%** of those aware follow the account
- **38.1%** watch content 3-5 times per week
- **19.3%** watch daily

### 2. Content Preferences & Engagement
- Most watched topics: **SRHR (65.9%)**, HIV/PrEP/PEP (64.8%), Mental Health (56.3%)
- Preferred formats: Short videos (54.8%), Live sessions (19.3%)
- **73.7%** like content; **35.6%** comment or share
- **32.2%** have asked health questions through the platform

### 3. Trust & Safety
- **96.3%** trust the health information provided
- **95.6%** feel safe and respected on the platform
- **91.9%** comfortable asking health questions

### 4. Knowledge & Behavior Change
- **97.0%** report increased health knowledge
- **95.6%** encouraged to seek services when needed
- **95.6%** more confident making health decisions
- Awareness of youth-friendly services increased from **57.4% to 94.1%** (+36.7 pp)

### 5. Service Referral & Utilization
- **40.7%** of followers were referred to services
- Most referred services: Mental Health (48.2%), HIV testing (47.3%), Youth Clinics (45.5%)
- **96.4%** of those referred accessed services
- **100%** found services helpful or very helpful

### 6. Barriers to Access
Among those who did not access services:
- **Distance**: 28.6%
- **Lack of time**: 26.3%
- **Privacy concerns**: 24.1%
- **Fear or stigma**: 16.5%
- **Cost**: 15.0%
- **Parental concerns**: 7.5%
